Before you go:

  • DO NOT TRAVEL TO CAMPGROUNDS, PARKS OR OTHER RESERVES IF ANY PERSON IN YOUR TRAVELLING PARTY:
  • may have COVID-19 coronavirus symptoms
  • may have had close contact with a person who has COVID-19 coronavirus
  • is awaiting the results of a COVID-19 test
  • has been advised to self-isolate by a health professional
  • ensure you are self-sufficient and able to maintain good personal hygiene by carrying your own handwashing equipment and materials: facilities at Parks and Wildlife Service campgrounds are basic
  • Check:
    • WA government information and advice on COVID-19 including travel and gathering restrictions
    • Parks and Wildlife Service’s Explore Parks website and Park Alerts
    • The Bureau of Meteorology for weather forecasts and warnings Emergency WA for warnings and incidents
    • The ABC radio frequency for the area you are visiting (ABC local radio broadcasts emergency information)
    • Your contact details and vehicle registration are correct

At the campground and park:

  • Practise appropriate social distancing including:
  • minimising all unnecessary contact with others
  • keeping 1.5 metres away from others
  • avoiding physical greetings such as handshakes, hug and kisses
  • The 4m² space per person rule must be observed at all times
  • Cover your mouth and nose when you cough or sneeze. Use a flexed elbow or a tissue - if you use a tissue, dispose of it immediately and appropriately.
